The Story

A modern-day folk story, 'And We Fell' explores a couple's pursuit of redemption for themselves.

Abandoned by their families and plagued by past misdeeds, Ben Garber and Samantha Ackert can only rely on each other. Against the threat of violence, the two concoct a dangerous scheme to rob the home of Ben's employer and escape their hard-luck town. The surprise appearance of a six-year old child forces the couple to revaluate their dreams.

Director's Statement

Why are we telling this story?


One word: Family. That single word brings so many emotions to the surface for people, inciting laughter, pain, anger, joy, etc. All of us harbor very personal stories about our families. These unique and complex relationships with family members shape who we are and how we perceive the world around us. We want to share a story that is willing to explore the messier side of family dynamics. Often we strive to embrace familial relationships, but sometimes we find ourselves running away from them.

This is very true for our main characters, Ben and Samantha. They are people who desperately want to lead honest lives but are deeply influenced by the failings of the men and women who brought them into the world. Ben longs to escape the sins of his father. Samantha looks to her grandmother for inspiration due to the absence of both her parents.

We want to tell a story about family that isn’t cookie-cutter or neat. ‘And We Fell’ is grounded in reality. It is complex and raw. And it allows for the characters to be flawed and emotional. Please help us to tell this story, which reflects an affecting and unique perspective on family.
